Neuron Simulator

Synapse types

  • Green = excitatory (EPSP)
  • Red = inhibitory (IPSP)

Single synapse controls

  • Hover over a synapse to reveal controls
  • ➕ / ➖ adjust synaptic strength
  • Click a synapse to release neurotransmitter

Group firing

  • Hold Control and click synapses to select them
  • Selected synapses are highlighted
  • Press Space to fire all selected synapses together
  • Small postsynaptic potentials (PSPs) may decay before reaching the soma, especially when synapses are weak or distant.
  • Large or cooperative excitatory PSPs can summate and drive the membrane potential toward action potential threshold.
  • Inhibitory PSPs counteract depolarization and can suppress action potential generation even in the presence of excitation.
  • Multiple synapses interact dynamically, allowing competition or cooperation across dendritic branches.
  • Astrocytes respond to synaptic activity, reflecting their role in sensing neurotransmitter release and modulating local signaling.
  • Neural firing increases metabolic demand, triggering activity-dependent neurovascular coupling.
  • Blood vessels respond to neural activity with pulsatile flow and pressure waves linked to the heartbeat.
  • Oxygen dissociates from hemoglobin and, along with glucose, accumulates in the perivascular space before delivery.
  • Transport across the blood–brain barrier is selective and activity-dependent, conceptually reflecting channels such as AQP4 (water) and GLUT1 (glucose).
  • Following neuronal firing, increased oxygen and glucose availability supports sustained signaling and recovery of membrane potential.
